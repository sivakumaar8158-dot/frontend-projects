1. Passport Regulations


Citizenship Requirement: Only recognized citizens of the Republic of Lunara may apply for a Lunarian passport. Proof of birth or citizenship certificate is mandatory.

Passport Validity: Standard Passport: Valid for 10 years. Temporary or Emergency Passport: Valid for 1 year.

Passport Renewal: Renewal applications must be submitted 6 months prior to expiry, along with updated biometric data.

Lost or Stolen Passports: Citizens must immediately report lost or stolen passports to the Lunarian Department of Immigration and Security (LDIS). Replacement requires verification and an official police report.

Dual Citizenship Policy: The Republic of Lunara allows dual citizenship only with countries having mutual agreements and trust treaties with Lunara.





2. Visa Regulations


Visa Requirement: All foreign visitors must obtain a valid visa before entering Lunara, unless exempted by international agreement. Visa Categories: Tourist Visa: For sightseeing, leisure, and short stays (up to 90 days). Business Visa: For trade, investment, or business conferences (up to 180 days). Student Visa: For academic programs or research at recognized Lunarian institutions. Work Visa: Granted to skilled professionals with official employment approval. Diplomatic Visa: For government or official delegates. Transit Visa: For travelers passing through Lunara (valid up to 48 hours).

Visa-Free Access: Citizens of partner nations under the Lunarian Peace Accord may visit without a visa for up to 60 days.

Entry Requirements: Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity). Proof of return or onward travel. Sufficient funds for stay. Health and security clearance if required.

Extension & Overstay Rules: Visitors may apply for extension up to 30 days. Overstaying a visa is considered a violation of national law and may result in fines, deportation, or entry bans.

Visa Denial: The government reserves the right to deny or revoke a visa if the applicant poses a security, health, or moral risk to the Republic.
